The pattern of moisture damage (rising dampness, efflorescence, and cooler thermal signature at the base of the wall) strongly suggests **rising damp** due to a failed or inadequate damp-proof course (DPC) allowing capillary action to draw moisture from the ground/slab into the wall. Alternatively, a localized **hidden plumbing leak** within the wall or floor at ground level, or a **waterproofing failure from an adjacent wet area** (such as a bathroom, which other photos in the appendix suggest might have issues), could also be contributing factors, allowing moisture to migrate into this internal wall.

**5. Severity Assessment:**
**Medium-High**
*   **Reasoning:** The damage is visually significant, indicating active and prolonged moisture ingress. The thermal imaging confirms the presence of moisture within the wall, which can lead to progressive deterioration of plaster and paint, potential mold growth (posing health risks and further material damage), and compromise indoor air quality. If left unaddressed, repair costs will escalate, and the integrity of finishes will continue to degrade. While not immediately structural, persistent dampness can affect building materials over time.

**6. Recommended Actions:**
1.  **Comprehensive Moisture Investigation:** Conduct an intrusive moisture survey using a professional moisture meter to accurately map the extent of moisture saturation within the wall and adjacent floor areas. Simultaneously, investigate potential external sources such as ground levels against the building, external render condition, and the integrity of the DPC. Internally, inspect adjacent plumbing lines (if accessible) and waterproofing in any adjoining wet areas.
2.  **Source Remediation:** Once the specific source of moisture is definitively identified (e.g., failed DPC, external drainage issue, hidden pipe leak, or wet area waterproofing failure), implement immediate and appropriate remedial actions. This may involve installing a new chemical DPC, repairing external defects, fixing plumbing leaks, or undertaking re-waterproofing works in adjacent wet zones.
3.  **Drying & Restoration:** After the moisture source has been fully rectified, allow the affected wall to thoroughly dry out. This may require the use of dehumidifiers and controlled ventilation. Once dry, remove all damaged plaster and paint back to the sound substrate. Treat any areas affected by mold (if present) with an appropriate fungicide, then re-plaster and repaint using moisture-resistant materials suitable for the environment.
